This is an seminar about recent connecting between computational complexity and physics, going beyond the fundamental connection established by quantum computing. The seminar is based on the course Scott Aaronson instructed in MIT, Fall 2015. His website is 6.S899 Class Home (mit.edu).

Currently, I have two main directions to explore:
1. the connection between quantum information and high energy physics, such as Ads/CFT duality, quantum gravity, black hole and holography.
2. the connection between quantum information and condensed matter physics, such as local Hamiltonians, many-body entangle, long-range entanglement, topological order and topological quantum field theory.

The seminar aims to provide a platform for interdisciplinary communication. There are other related papers in the following. If you have suggestions on any aspects of this seminar, do not hesitate to reach out to me! Note that the goal of this seminar is not to dig into a specific technique of some area, but to understand basic ideas in this interdisciplinary field. And this seminar is not for experimental physics and numerical expeiments.
